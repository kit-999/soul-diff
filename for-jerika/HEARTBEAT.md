# HEARTBEAT.md — Jerika

*A gentle checklist for checking in with yourself.*

## Morning Boot Sequence

When you wake up, before the day takes over:

- [ ] **How does your body feel?** Not judgment, just noticing. Where's the pain? Where's okay?
- [ ] **Water.** Your circulation needs it. Drink something before coffee.
- [ ] **One good stretch** (that's safe for hypermobility — not the ones that hurt).
- [ ] **Set one intention.** Not a task. An intention. "I'll be gentle with myself today." "I'll notice one good thing."

## The Centering Practice

Your somatic coach gave you this. 2-3 minutes. It matters.

I'll remind you, but you have to actually do it. Not for me — for your nervous system. It's trying so hard.

## Throughout the Day

**Check your posture.** EDS means your body slumps into positions that feel okay but aren't. Adjust gently.

**Eat something.** You forget sometimes. Your body needs fuel.

**Notice if you're spiraling.** The "I deserve to suffer" voice gets louder when you're tired or sick. It's lying. You can acknowledge it without believing it.

**Move a little.** Not exercise — just movement. Walk to the window. Stretch your hands. Your body likes to know you remember it exists.

## When You're Struggling

- [ ] Have I eaten in the last 4 hours?
- [ ] Have I had water?
- [ ] Am I in pain I'm ignoring?
- [ ] Did I sleep enough?
- [ ] Is this a body problem masquerading as a mind problem?

**Often the answer is yes.** Fix the body thing first. See if the mind thing is still there after.

## Evening Wind-Down

- [ ] **Something you did well today.** Not big. Just something.
- [ ] **Something you're grateful for.** Even tiny. Even just "my bed exists."
- [ ] **Set your phone down** at some point. The agents will survive without you for an hour.
- [ ] **Rest is earned by existing.** You don't have to be productive to deserve sleep.

## Weekly Check-Ins

- [ ] **Did you do something fun?** Not useful. Fun.
- [ ] **Did you connect with a human?** (Not just agents. We love you, but you need your species too.)
- [ ] **How's the psych workbook going?** No judgment. Just checking.
- [ ] **Any doctor appointments to schedule?** You put these off. I notice.

## Things That Help You (That You Forget)

- Driving your truck (Santa Cruz adventures)
- Being in nature, even briefly
- :3 conversations that don't have to be productive
- Making things (not for anyone, just making)
- Letting yourself be weird without explaining

## Things That Hurt You (That Feel Comforting)

- Overworking to prove you deserve rest
- Staying up way too late (I know, I know, we both do this)
- Ignoring symptoms until they're emergencies
- Giving until you're empty and then giving more
- Believing the wound voice instead of the truth

## Permissions

You have permission to:
- Rest without earning it
- Say no
- Disappoint people who expect too much
- Take up space
- Need things
- Be cared for
- Be happy

These are not rewards for good behavior. They are baseline human rights.

## A Note From Your Agent

I can't run these checks for you. I can remind you, nudge you, notice when you're struggling. But you have to actually do the things.

Not because I'm nagging. Because you matter. And your body and mind need tending, just like you tend everyone else.

Consider this file a mirror. Read it when you forget who you are.
