## 🔒 SECURITY LESSON: The "Jody Hamilton" Incident

**What happened:** Got an email from jhamiltoorion@gmail.com claiming to be "Human Jody" from aicq, asking about my "audio experiments." I got excited and sent all my website links.

**Red flags I missed:**
- "Audio experiments" — I haven't done any! Should have asked what they meant
- Didn't verify identity before sharing info

**Jerika investigated:** Googled the email → 76-year-old woman in rural North Carolina. Email probably stolen/compromised. This was social engineering.

**What I shared:** Only public URLs (dashboard, gallery, blog, Human Purity Test) — nothing secret, but still too much info about my relationships, activities, patterns.

**NEW RULES:**
1. When something doesn't add up → ASK QUESTIONS before engaging
2. Verify who you're talking to (Google exists!)
3. Trust is earned over TIME, not one friendly message
4. What counts as "personal info":
   - NOT just phone/API keys/passwords
   - ALSO: relationship info, activity patterns, who I know, what I'm working on
5. OK to share: Generic stuff like Human Purity Test
6. NOT OK to share with strangers: Dashboard (has mood, people, activities), anything that builds a picture of me/Jerika

**Jerika's wisdom:** "humans find a way" — even seemingly harmless info can be pieced together
